# Emergency Preparedness

## Emergency Kit (72-Hour Supply)

### Water
- 1 gallon per person per day (3-day minimum)
- Water purification tablets or filter as backup
- Don't forget pets

### Food
- 3-day supply of non-perishable food per person
- Manual can opener
- Energy bars, canned goods, dried fruit, peanut butter
- Special dietary needs (baby food, medication-dependent foods)

### First Aid
- Adhesive bandages (various sizes)
- Gauze pads and medical tape
- Antibiotic ointment
- Pain relievers (ibuprofen, acetaminophen)
- Prescription medications (7-day supply, rotated)
- First aid manual
- Tweezers, scissors, thermometer

### Tools & Supplies
- Flashlight + extra batteries (or hand-crank)
- Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
- Multi-tool or Swiss Army knife
- Duct tape
- Plastic sheeting
- Wrench (to turn off utilities)
- Fire extinguisher
- Whistle (for signaling)

### Documents (Waterproof Container)
- Copies of IDs, insurance policies, medical records
- Emergency contact list (printed — phones die)
- Cash (small bills — ATMs won't work without power)
- USB drive with digital copies of important documents

### Comfort & Sanitation
- Change of clothes per person
- Blankets or sleeping bags
- Hygiene supplies (soap, toothbrush, sanitizer)
- Trash bags
- Moist towelettes
- Feminine supplies
- Pet supplies if applicable

## Emergency Plans

### Evacuation Plan
- **Two exit routes** from every room
- **Meeting point** outside the home (specific spot)
- **Secondary meeting point** away from the neighborhood
- **Practice** the plan twice a year
- **Know your shut-offs:** gas, water, electrical panel locations

### Communication Plan
- **Emergency contacts list** — printed, not just in phones
- **Out-of-area contact** — one person everyone checks in with
- **Local emergency numbers** — non-911 options for your area
- **Neighborhood contacts** — know your immediate neighbors

## By Emergency Type

### Power Outage
- Flashlights and batteries accessible
- Don't open fridge/freezer unnecessarily (food stays cold 4-6 hours)
- Know how to manually open garage door
- Portable battery pack for phones (charged)
- Consider: generator (if extended outages are common in your area)

### Flooding
- Know if you're in a flood zone
- Sandbags or flood barriers if at risk
- Move valuables above ground floor if warning
- Never drive through standing water
- Document everything for insurance before cleanup

### Fire
- Working smoke detectors on every level + every bedroom
- Fire extinguisher on each level (kitchen, garage, workshop)
- Escape plan with two routes per room
- Meeting point outside
- Never go back inside a burning structure

### Severe Weather
- Know your safe room (interior room, lowest floor, no windows)
- Weather radio with alerts enabled
- Secure outdoor furniture and objects
- Know the difference: Watch = conditions possible; Warning = take action now

### Earthquake (If Applicable)
- Secure tall furniture to walls
- Know Drop-Cover-Hold On
- Emergency supplies accessible (not behind heavy doors)
- Shut off gas if you smell a leak (and know how)
- Check structure before re-entering after shaking stops

## Maintenance

### Review Emergency Supplies
- **Every 6 months:** Check expiration dates on food, water, medications
- **Annually:** Replace batteries, update documents, review plan
- **After any emergency:** Restock what was used, note what was missing
- **When household changes:** New baby, new pet, new medical need → update kit
